The Story of Charles de Gaulle

Charles de Gaulle was a towering figure in French history, known for his leadership during World War II and his role in establishing the Fifth Republic of France. His resilience and strategic vision played a crucial role in shaping modern France.

Timeline of Key Events

  • 1890: Birth

    Charles de Gaulle was born on November 22, 1890, in Lille, France. His early life was marked by a strong sense of duty and nationalism.

  • 1940: Appeal of June 18

    After the fall of France to Germany, de Gaulle made his famous Appeal of June 18, 1940, calling for French resistance against Nazi occupation and leading the Free French Forces from London.

  • 1944: Liberation of France

    De Gaulle's leadership was pivotal in the liberation of France. He returned to Paris on August 25, 1944, to a hero’s welcome as the head of the Provisional Government of the French Republic.

  • 1958: Founding of the Fifth Republic

    In 1958, de Gaulle was instrumental in the creation of the Fifth Republic and became its first President, restoring stability to France amidst political turmoil.

  • 1969: Resignation

    De Gaulle resigned from the presidency on April 28, 1969, following a failed referendum on institutional reforms. His resignation marked the end of his second term in office.

  • 1970: Death

    Charles de Gaulle died on November 9, 1970, in Colombey-les-Deux-Églises. His legacy endures as a symbol of French strength and resilience.

Legacy

Charles de Gaulle's influence extends beyond his wartime leadership. As a key architect of modern France, he left a lasting impact on French politics, military strategy, and national identity.
