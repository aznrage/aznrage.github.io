The Story of King Louis XIII

King Louis XIII, known for his reign during a tumultuous period in French history, worked closely with his chief minister, Cardinal Richelieu, to strengthen the monarchy and centralize power.

Timeline of Key Events

  • 1601: Birth

    Louis XIII was born on September 27, 1601, as the son of King Henry IV and Marie de' Medici.

  • 1610: Accession to the Throne

    Following the assassination of his father, Louis XIII ascended to the throne at the age of nine, with his mother acting as regent until he came of age.

  • 1624: Rise of Cardinal Richelieu

    Cardinal Richelieu was appointed chief minister, and his policies significantly influenced the course of Louis XIII's reign.

  • 1643: Death

    Louis XIII died on May 14, 1643. His reign is noted for the strengthening of royal authority and the consolidation of the French state.

Legacy

Louis XIII's reign is characterized by his collaboration with Cardinal Richelieu, which greatly enhanced the power of the French monarchy and set the stage for Louis XIV's rule.
