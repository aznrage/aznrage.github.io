The Story of King Louis X

King Louis X, known as Louis the Quarrelsome, was a king of France during the early 14th century whose reign was marked by conflict and attempts to strengthen the monarchy.

Timeline of Key Events

  • 1289: Birth

    Louis X was born on October 4, 1289, as the son of King Philip IV and Queen Jeanne I of Navarre.

  • 1314: Accession to the Throne

    Following the death of his father, Louis X became king. His reign was characterized by internal conflicts and efforts to consolidate his power.

  • 1315: The Great Famine

    The Great Famine began affecting France during Louis X's reign, leading to widespread hardship and unrest.

  • 1316: Death

    King Louis X died on June 5, 1316. His reign was short but notable for the challenges faced and the impact on the French monarchy.

Legacy

Louis X's reign, although brief, was significant due to the political and social challenges he faced. His efforts to address these issues left a mark on the history of France.
