The Story of Harry S. Truman

Harry S. Truman, the 33rd President of the United States, assumed office in 1945 and led the country through the final stages of World War II and the beginning of the Cold War. His leadership was pivotal in shaping the post-war world.

Timeline of Key Events

  • 1884: Birth

    Harry S. Truman was born on May 8, 1884, in Lamar, Missouri.

  • 1944: Vice Presidency

    Truman was elected Vice President of the United States under President Franklin D. Roosevelt.

  • 1945: Ascension to Presidency

    Truman became President on April 12, 1945, following Roosevelt's death.

  • 1945: Atomic Bombings

    Truman authorized the use of atomic bombs on Hiroshima and Nagasaki in August 1945, leading to Japan's surrender and the end of World War II.

  • 1947: Truman Doctrine

    Truman announced the Truman Doctrine in March 1947, providing economic and military aid to countries threatened by communism, marking the beginning of the Cold War.

  • 1952: End of Presidency

    Truman left office on January 20, 1953, succeeded by Dwight D. Eisenhower. He retired to Missouri, where he continued to be active in public life.

Legacy

Harry S. Truman's presidency is noted for its decisive actions in the final days of World War II and its early Cold War policies. His leadership during a transformative period in history left a lasting impact on U.S. foreign policy and global relations.
