The Story of King Louis V

King Louis V, known as Louis the Do-Nothing, had a brief and largely uneventful reign. His rule was marked by a lack of significant achievements or impact on the realm.

Timeline of Key Events

  • 967: Birth

    Louis V was born in 967 as the son of King Lothair of France. His early life was relatively quiet and uneventful.

  • 986: Accession to the Throne

    Upon the death of his father, Louis V became King of France. His reign began during a period of political instability and external threats.

  • 987: Death

    Louis V's reign lasted only a short time before he died on May 21, 987. His death led to the rise of Hugh Capet, who would establish a new dynasty.

Legacy

King Louis V's reign is often considered insignificant due to its brevity and lack of notable achievements. His death marked the end of the Carolingian dynasty and the beginning of the Capetian dynasty in France.
