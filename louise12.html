The Story of King Louis XII

King Louis XII, known as the Father of the People, is remembered for his efforts to strengthen France and his active involvement in the Italian Wars.

Timeline of Key Events

  • 1462: Birth

    Louis XII was born on June 27, 1462, as the son of Charles, Duke of Orléans, and Marie of Cleves.

  • 1498: Accession to the Throne

    Following the death of Charles VIII, Louis XII became king of France. His reign focused on reforming the legal system and expanding French influence in Italy.

  • 1515: Battle of Marignano

    Louis XII led a successful campaign in Italy, securing important territories for France and enhancing his reputation as a military leader.

  • 1515: Death

    King Louis XII died on January 1, 1515. His reign is noted for the consolidation of French power and legal reforms.

Legacy

Louis XII's reign is celebrated for his reforms, military successes, and efforts to improve the lives of his subjects. His impact on French history was significant and enduring.
