The Story of King Louis VII

King Louis VII, also known as Louis the Young, was a medieval monarch who played a key role in the development of the French monarchy and its involvement in the Second Crusade.

Timeline of Key Events

  • 1120: Birth

    Louis VII was born on August 5, 1120, as the son of Louis VI and Adelaide of Savoy.

  • 1137: Accession to the Throne

    Following the death of his father, Louis VII ascended to the throne. His reign began with various internal and external challenges.

  • 1147-1149: Second Crusade

    Louis VII participated in the Second Crusade, aiming to strengthen Christian presence in the Holy Land. The campaign ultimately ended in failure.

  • 1180: Death

    King Louis VII died on September 18, 1180. His reign was marked by efforts to strengthen royal power and influence both within and outside France.

Legacy

Louis VII's reign had a significant impact on the French monarchy, particularly through his involvement in the Crusades and his efforts to consolidate royal power. His reign set the stage for future developments in France's political landscape.
