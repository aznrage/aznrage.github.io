The Story of King Louis XVII

King Louis XVII, though never officially crowned, was a symbolic figure during the French Revolution, representing the continuity of the monarchy during a period of significant upheaval.

Timeline of Key Events

  • 1785: Birth

    Louis XVII was born on March 27, 1785, as the second son of King Louis XVI and Queen Marie Antoinette.

  • 1792: Declared King

    Following the abdication of his father in 1792, Louis XVII was declared king, though he never officially ruled.

  • 1795: Death

    Louis XVII died on June 8, 1795, in captivity, a victim of the harsh conditions and political turmoil of the French Revolution.

Legacy

Louis XVII's short life and symbolic role during the Revolution highlight the tragic end of the French monarchy and the tumultuous times that shaped modern France.
