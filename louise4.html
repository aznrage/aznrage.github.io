The Story of King Louis IV

King Louis IV, also known as Louis d'Outremer, was a medieval monarch whose reign was marked by efforts to restore stability to a fragmented realm. His reign was characterized by the struggle to reassert control over a kingdom plagued by external threats and internal dissent.

Timeline of Key Events

  • 921: Birth

    Louis IV was born on April 10, 921, in the Frankish kingdom. He was the son of Charles the Simple, the King of West Francia.

  • 923: Accession to the Throne

    After the deposition of his father, Louis IV was elected King of West Francia. His reign began during a tumultuous period with various regional conflicts.

  • 925: Struggles with Nobility

    Louis IV faced significant opposition from powerful nobles who sought to undermine his authority. His reign was marked by continuous struggles to consolidate power.

  • 931: Treaty with the Vikings

    In an effort to secure peace, Louis IV negotiated a treaty with Viking leaders. This was part of his strategy to stabilize his reign and protect his kingdom.

  • 954: Death

    King Louis IV died on September 10, 954. His death led to a brief period of instability before the succession of his successors.

Legacy

King Louis IV's reign was marked by significant challenges, including internal conflict and external threats. Despite these difficulties, his efforts to restore order had a lasting impact on the political landscape of medieval France.
