The Story of King Louis XIV

King Louis XIV, known as the Sun King, is celebrated for his long and influential reign, during which he centralized power and established the absolute monarchy in France.

Timeline of Key Events

  • 1638: Birth

    Louis XIV was born on September 5, 1638, as the son of King Louis XIII and Anne of Austria.

  • 1643: Accession to the Throne

    Upon the death of his father, Louis XIV ascended to the throne at the age of four, with his mother acting as regent until he came of age.

  • 1661: Direct Rule

    Louis XIV began his personal rule, taking direct control of the French government and centralizing power in his own hands.

  • 1682: Versailles

    Louis XIV moved the royal court to the Palace of Versailles, which became the center of political power in France.

  • 1715: Death

    King Louis XIV died on September 1, 1715, after a reign of 72 years, leaving a lasting legacy on French history and governance.

Legacy

Louis XIV's reign is noted for the consolidation of royal power and the establishment of the absolute monarchy. His policies and cultural patronage had a profound impact on France and European history.
